Payment Posting In Medical Billing

At Vitalink India, payment posting in medical billing is a crucial process that receives top priority from our Operations management. We ensure that payments received from payers and patients in relation to claims are accurately posted in the client's medical billing system for claim reconciliation. Additionally, Vitalink also handles electronic payment posting into the medical billing software and manually manages any exceptions or fallouts to ensure that no payments are missed. Insurance Payment Posting

At Vitalink India, payment posting in medical billing is a crucial process that receives top priority from our Operations management. We ensure that payments received from payers and patients in relation to claims are accurately posted in the client's medical billing system for claim reconciliation. Additionally, Vitalink also handles electronic payment posting into the medical billing software and manually manages any exceptions or fallouts to ensure that no payments are missed. The posted electronic remittance advices (ERAs) are securely stored either in the billing system or a Document Management System (DMS) for future reference.

However, if the client's office delays depositing Payer checks or sending ERAs and EOBs for posting, it can create a negative balance for that claim. This negative balance inaccurately represents the actual situation and can lead to an inflated accounts receivable (AR). As a result, physicians are unable to determine the exact amount of revenue owed to them.

Patient Cash Posting

Patients may be responsible for paying a portion of the expenses for various reasons, such as co-pays, deductibles, and non-covered services. When the amount owed by the patients is minimal, the Provider may choose to write off the balance. However, if the amount is significant, it should be collected from the patients either before or after the services are rendered.

Patients usually make payments using checks or credit cards through patient portals. It is crucial to accurately account for these payments against the claim to prevent any inflated accounts receivable (AR) and ensure the proper closure of the claim.
